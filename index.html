Auditory training, for free

Speech Banana is a training application for cochlear implant users, designed to build the skills needed for understanding everyday speech.

Try the iOS app today

Motivation

Learning to interpret the signals provided by a cochlear implant is an extensive and ongoing process, often taking months to years for a patient to master complex tasks such as speech comprehension, and partly dependent on how often they can practice using their device. Auditory training (AT) programs, which allow patients to build up the individual listening skills needed to understand spoken communication, are critically beneficial to this learning process.

Despite the benefits of AT and the ongoing nature of auditory learning, patients in many countries face considerable barriers to accessing regular sessions, including limited coverage by insurance and limited availability of clinical training programs. Computerized training applications thus offer a useful supplement to in-person training, filling in the gaps between less frequent trips to the clinic.

Many such applications are now available for children but only in recent months have a few emerged for adults. We now offer our own app, Speech Banana, in the hopes of providing a modular, functional and user-friendly at-home training tool for adults with CIs.

How it works

Chapters

Build your listeining skills over 38 lessons, organized in 7 chapters.

Lessons

Each lesson will present you with different sets of words containing contrasting sounds, priming you to pick up on their differences in real speech.

Quizzes

After each lesson, you'll test your skills by listening to real sentences containing the sounds you just learned and writing down what you hear.

Track your progress

Look up your old scores and watch your skills grow.

Lesson Plan

The lesson plan used in Speech Banana was originally developed by Whitehurst and Monsees, published in their 1952 book, Auditory Training for the Deaf. Our team chose this lesson plan because it is freely available online via the Hathi Trust Digital Library. We have confirmed the plan's effectiveness with several audiologists and speech language specialists.

The lessons cover all basic English language phonemes and increase in difficulty as chapters progress from easier, lower frequency phonemes to harder, higher frequency ones. In this way, we cover the speech banana, the entire spectrum of sounds that we hear in most spoken communication.

Our Team

Speech Banana is a project originally developed by three undergraduate students at Johns Hopkins University and advised by Dr. Ratnanather at the Center for Imaging Science. The team has since grown to include a few more members who are helping to expand the app to new platforms and languages and with the development of new features like more specific quiz feedback.

John Tilak Ratnanather

Dr. Ratnanather is an associate research professor in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. The Speech Banana project stems from his experience as a deaf adult who has used a cochlear implant for almost three years and benefited from clinical auditory training sessions, which are considered essential post-surgery but rarely covered by insurance. We aim to address this problem by bringing auditory training to adults unable to attend regular sessions in the clinic or denied insurance coverage.
